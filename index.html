WELCOME TO PROJECT PHYLOGEN

PHYLOGEN is a multi-platform assistive application for Biology professionals, students, and researchers in the field of phylogenetics. Specifically, it is a data interface that assists in performing phylogenetic analysis, either by morphological or sequential data, through the widely used NEXUS file format.

For those unfamiliar with phylogenetics, it is the study of the evolutionary history and relationships among organisms (e.g. individual or groups of species, or populations). These relationships are discovered through phylogenetic "inference" methods that evaluate/compare observed heritable traits, such as DNA sequences or morphology under a model of evolution of these traits. The result of these analyses is a phylogeny (also known as a phylogenetic tree) – a hypothesis about the possible history of the organisms' evolutionary relationships.

There are several widely-used programs that take care of the complex inference methods involved in creating phylogenies. Some include PAUP, PHYLIP, TreeGen, Dendroscope, and Archaeopteryx. These applications perform their complex operations well and have done so for many years, however their methods of acquiring, editing, and transferring data are outdated. PHYLOGEN attempts to bridge the gap between these tried and true phylogeny-creation wares and the modern expectations users have of data-heavy program UIs.
